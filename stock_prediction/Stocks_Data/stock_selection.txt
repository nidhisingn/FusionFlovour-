======================================================================
                    STOCK SELECTION — STEP-BY-STEP PLAYBOOK
======================================================================

Objective:
  Move from “ideas” -> “a small set of high-quality stocks” using a repeatable
  process: Sector -> Screen -> Qualitative -> Valuation -> Buy plan.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — START WITH WHAT YOU ALREADY KNOW (Peter Lynch rule)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Before looking at any numbers, ask yourself:
  • What industry do you work in?
  • What products do you use daily?
  • What services do you pay for every month?
  • What businesses do you see growing around you?

Why this matters:
  You already have ground-level qualitative knowledge about industries you live
  inside. You can often see changes before analysts do.

You might already know:
  • Which product is better
  • Which brand is gaining popularity
  • Which service people are switching to
  • What problems exist that someone is solving

Example:
  If you work in IT — you may know who is winning deals, which technologies are
  in demand, and which companies employees prefer. That is an edge even before
  you open Screener.in.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — SECTOR SELECTION FILTER (if you're unsure)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

FILTER 1 — TAILWIND CHECK
  Is this sector growing faster than India’s GDP (~6–7%)?
    - YES -> Proceed
    - NO  -> Skip unless valuations are very cheap

FILTER 2 — UNDERSTANDING CHECK
  Do you understand how companies in this sector make money?
    - YES -> Proceed
    - NO  -> Learn first or skip

FILTER 3 — CYCLE CHECK
  Where is the sector in its cycle?
    - Early/Growth -> Best time to enter
    - Mature       -> Prefer best-in-class only
    - Declining    -> Avoid (unless special situation)

FILTER 4 — CURRENT SENTIMENT CHECK
  Is the sector beaten down or overheated?
    - Beaten down + fundamentals intact -> Opportunity
    - Overheated + everyone excited     -> Be cautious

Contrarian principle:
  Best time to study a sector is when nobody is talking about it.
  When everyone is excited, valuations stretch; when ignored, value hides.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — STOCK SELECTION WITHIN A SECTOR (3-level funnel)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

LEVEL 1 — QUANTITATIVE SCREEN (eliminate weak companies)
  Use Screener.in with these baseline filters:
    • Revenue growth  > 10% (last 3 years)
    • Net profit growth > 10%
    • ROE             > 15%
    • D/E             < 1.5
    • Free Cash Flow  > 0

  Result:
    This removes ~70% of stocks. You should end up with ~5–8 candidates.

LEVEL 2 — QUALITATIVE FILTER (pick the best 2–3)
  Apply the “6 pillars” framework:
    • Strongest moat?
    • Best management?
    • Gaining market share?
    • Least business risk?

LEVEL 3 — VALUATION CHECK (buy at the right price)
  Compare:
    • P/E vs historical average
    • P/E vs sector peers
    • 52-week position
    • PEG ratio

Final pick:
  Choose 1–2 stocks with best combination of:
    QUALITY + GROWTH + REASONABLE VALUATION


----------------------------------------------------------------------
Screener.in — QUICK SCREEN TEMPLATE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Open: https://screener.in/screen

Use these filters:
  Sales growth 3Years      > 10
  Profit growth 3Years     > 10
  Return on equity         > 15
  Debt to equity           < 1
  Free cash flow           > 0

Then:
  • From results, filter by sector
  • Do deeper work on the top 5–8 companies


----------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTOR ROTATION (Economic cycle cheat-sheet)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Different sectors perform at different points of the economic cycle.

Early Recovery
  -> Banking, Auto, Real Estate
     (rates falling, borrowing starts, demand improves)

Growth Phase
  -> IT, Consumer Goods, Industrials
     (business investment + consumer spending strong)

Late Cycle
  -> Energy, Commodities, Metals
     (inflation rising, raw material demand peaks)

Recession / Slowdown
  -> Pharma, FMCG, IT Services
     (defensives hold up better)

India context (2025–26):
  Rates easing -> Early recovery bias -> Banking + consumption may benefit.
  IT may be in temporary slowdown waiting for US recovery.
