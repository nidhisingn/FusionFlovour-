======================================================================
                   FUNDAMENTAL STOCK SELECTION PLAYBOOK
======================================================================

Purpose:
  A simple, repeatable checklist to move from “stock ideas” -> “good decisions”.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
1) THE DECISION TREE (End-to-End)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 0 — Start with a sector shortlist
  • Analyze one sector at a time
  • Rank stocks inside that sector

Step 1 — Fundamental Strength?
  • Is the top pick fundamentally strong?
      - YES  -> Continue
      - NO   -> Skip and go to the next ranked stock

Step 2 — Valuation Reasonable?
  • Is valuation reasonable?
      - YES (P/E near or below historical) -> BUY / ACCUMULATE
      - NO  (P/E very high)                -> WATCHLIST, wait for correction

Step 3 — Allocation (Position Sizing)
  • High conviction   -> 15–20%
  • Medium conviction -> 8–10%
  • Always keep cash  -> 15–20%

Step 4 — Entry Method
  • Clearly cheap     -> Lump sum
  • Uncertain timing  -> Stagger buys over ~3 months

Step 5 — Exit Plan (Set BEFORE Buying)
  • Target price  -> where you take profit
  • Stop loss     -> where you cut loss
  • Thesis break  -> what would change your mind

Step 6 — Monitoring (Quarterly)
  • Thesis intact  -> HOLD
  • Thesis broken  -> EXIT (regardless of price)
  • Price hit target -> PARTIAL / FULL exit


----------------------------------------------------------------------
2) THE MINDSET SHIFT (Most Important)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Most people think investing is about finding the right stock.
It’s actually about making the right decision at each stage.

Outcome drivers (equal weight):
  • Right stock       -> 20%
  • Right price       -> 20%
  • Right allocation  -> 20%
  • Right exit        -> 20%
  • Right mindset     -> 20%
      (stay calm during crashes, don’t panic sell)

Key message:
  Many people spend 100% of their energy on picking stocks, and 0% on the
  other 4 parts — that’s why they lose money even with good companies.
