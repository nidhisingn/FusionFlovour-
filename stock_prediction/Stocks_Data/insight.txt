PHASE 2 — BASIC CHART READING (Week 2–3)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

For short term trading you need basic technical analysis. Not complex — just enough to time entry and exit better.

---

The only 4 chart concepts you need

Concept 1 — Support and Resistance

RESISTANCE: price level where stock struggles to go above (sellers show up)
SUPPORT   : price level where stock stops falling (buyers show up)

Example levels (TCS):
  3,710  <- Resistance (52W high)
  3,200  <- Resistance
  2,613  <- Current price
  2,561  <- Support (52W low)
  2,400  <- Strong support below

Trading rule:
  • Buy near support (low risk; stop loss just below support)
  • Sell near resistance (take profit before sellers appear)

---

Concept 2 — Trend

UPTREND
  • Higher highs + higher lows -> buy dips

DOWNTREND
  • Lower highs + lower lows -> avoid buying

SIDEWAYS
  • Range-bound -> buy bottom of range, sell top

Golden rule:
  Never buy a falling stock hoping it will reverse.
  Wait for the trend to actually turn.

---

Concept 3 — Volume

Volume = number of shares traded in a day.

Price UP   + Volume UP    -> Strong move, genuine buying
Price UP   + Volume DOWN  -> Weak move, may not sustain
Price DOWN + Volume UP    -> Strong selling, avoid catching
Price DOWN + Volume DOWN  -> Weak selling, may stabilize

Whenever a news event happens — check if volume is unusually high. High volume confirms the move is real.

---

Concept 4 — Moving Averages (only two)

• 50 DMA  -> average price of last 50 days
• 200 DMA -> average price of last 200 days

Price above 200 DMA -> long-term uptrend
Price below 200 DMA -> long-term downtrend

Crossovers:
  • Price crosses above 50 DMA -> short-term momentum positive
  • Price crosses below 50 DMA -> short-term momentum negative

Patterns:
  • Golden Cross -> 50 DMA crosses above 200 DMA (bullish)
  • Death Cross  -> 50 DMA crosses below 200 DMA (bearish)

On TradingView — add these two indicators. Takes 30 seconds. Instantly tells you the trend context.

PHASE 4 — 5 TRADE SETUPS TO FOCUS ON
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Each setup has a clear entry trigger, target, and stop loss.

---

Setup 1 — Pre-Earnings Trade

**Logic:** Stock often moves before results as anticipation builds. If you expect strong results, buy 2-3 days before and sell on result day.

Process:
  Day -7:
    • Check last 4 quarters trend
    • Check estimates (Trendlyne)
    • Check management commentary
    • Check sector trend

  Day -3:
    • If everything positive -> buy 50% of planned position
    • Stop loss: 5% below entry

  Result day:
    • Beat estimates -> sell 50–75% into strength
    • In-line       -> hold and observe next-day reaction
    • Miss          -> exit fully

Targets:
  • Target    : 8–12%
  • Stop loss : 5% below entry
  • Time      : 3–7 days

**Example using TCS:**
```
TCS results expected March 13
Current price March 10 = ₹2,613
Entry = ₹2,613
Stop loss = ₹2,482 (5% below)
Target = ₹2,874 (10% above) if results strong
```

---

Setup 2 — Post-Earnings Reaction Trade

**Logic:** Strong results announced after hours. Stock will gap up next morning. Buy at open and ride the momentum.

```
PROCESS:

Evening — results announced:
→ Revenue beat? ✅
→ Net profit beat? ✅
→ Management guidance positive? ✅
→ All three yes → Plan to buy at market open tomorrow

Next morning:
→ Stock gaps up 3-5% at open
→ Wait 15 minutes after market opens (let initial volatility settle)
→ If stock holds the gap (doesn't fall back down) → BUY
→ If stock gaps up then immediately starts falling → SKIP this trade

Target: Additional 5-8% from your entry
Stop loss: Below the gap-up open level
Time frame: 1-5 days
```

**Why wait 15 minutes?**
At market open, retail panic buyers push price up. In 15 minutes this settles. If stock holds high after 15 minutes — the move is genuine. If it falls back — smart money is selling into retail excitement.

### Setup 3 — News Catalyst Trade (FDA/Deal/Regulatory)

**Logic:** A specific news event with predictable directional impact.

```
PHARMA — FDA Approval:
→ Stock jumps 15-30% same day
→ If you can buy before announcement → huge gain
→ If buying after announcement → buy only if:
  a) Move seems sustainable (not just spike and fall)
  b) This is first generic approval (180 day exclusivity)
  c) Volume is very high (confirming genuine interest)

IT — Large Deal Win:
→ Stock up 3-8% on announcement
→ Buy on announcement day
→ Target 5-8% over next 1 week
→ Stop loss if stock falls back to pre-announcement level

AUTO — Monthly Sales Data:
→ Every 1st of month, auto companies release monthly sales
→ If sales numbers strong → buy on that day
→ Quick 3-5% trade over 2-3 days
```

### Setup 5 — Oversold Bounce Trade

**Logic:** A fundamentally good stock falls 15-25% due to temporary bad news or market panic. Buy the overreaction, sell the recovery.

```
CRITERIA FOR THIS TRADE:
→ Stock must be fundamentally strong (from your watchlist)
→ Fall must be due to temporary issue not permanent damage
→ Stock must be near strong support level
→ RSI (momentum indicator) below 30 = oversold

EXAMPLE:
TCS falls from ₹2,800 to ₹2,200 because
US client cut one quarter's spending
→ This is temporary, not permanent business damage
→ RSI falls to 25 (deeply oversold)
→ Stock at strong support ₹2,200
→ BUY here

Target: ₹2,500-2,600 (recovery to mean)
Stop loss: ₹2,050 (below support)
Time frame: 2-6 weeks
```

**Most important question before this trade:**
Is the fall due to **temporary issue** or **permanent damage?**

```
TEMPORARY (good to buy):        PERMANENT (avoid):
One bad quarter                 Business model destroyed
Market-wide panic sell-off      Loss of major client forever
Commodity price spike           Regulatory ban on business
Global recession fears          Accounting fraud discovered
```

PHASE 5 — TRADE MANAGEMENT RULES (Non-negotiable)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

These rules are non-negotiable. Most traders lose money not because of bad stock picking but because of bad trade management.

---

Rule 1 — Position sizing

```
Total trading capital          = ₹1,00,000 (example)
Maximum per trade              = 10% = ₹10,000
Maximum open trades at once    = 4-5

Never put more than 10% in single trade.
Even if you're 100% sure — markets surprise everyone.
```

---

Rule 2 — Stop loss is mandatory

```
BEFORE entering any trade ask:
"Where is my stop loss?"

If you can't answer this → Don't enter the trade.

Stop loss for short term trades = 5-7% below entry
Stop loss for swing trades = 8-10% below entry

WHEN STOP LOSS HITS → EXIT IMMEDIATELY.
No hoping, no averaging down, no "it will come back."
```

The biggest losses in trading come from ignoring stop losses.

---

Rule 3 — Profit booking strategy

```
Don't try to catch the absolute top.

STAGGERED EXIT:
Hit 8% profit  → Sell 50% of position (secure partial profit)
Hit 15% profit → Sell another 30%
Let remaining 20% run with trailing stop loss

This way:
→ You always book some profit
→ You don't miss bigger moves
→ You never give back all gains
```

---

Rule 4 — The 3-trade rule

If you have 3 consecutive losing trades → **stop trading for 1 week.**

This forces you to review what went wrong instead of revenge trading (making impulsive trades to recover losses quickly — the fastest way to blow up capital).

---

Rule 5 — Never average down on short-term trades

```
LONG TERM INVESTING → Averaging down is okay
                      (buying more of a fundamentally 
                      strong stock when it falls)

SHORT TERM TRADING  → NEVER average down
                      If stock hits stop loss → exit
                      Don't buy more hoping it recovers
```

---

Rule 6 — Keep a trade journal

After every trade — write down:

```
Date         :
Stock        :
Entry price  :
Exit price   :
Profit/Loss  :
Why I entered:
What happened:
What I learned:
```

After 20-30 trades, patterns emerge. You'll see which setups work for you and which don't. This is how you improve — not by reading more theory but by reviewing your own trades.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 6 — MONTHLY REVIEW PROCESS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

```
EVERY MONTH END — 1 HOUR:

1. Total trades this month      : ___
2. Winning trades               : ___
3. Losing trades                : ___
4. Win rate %                   : ___  (target 50-60%)
5. Average profit per win       : ___  (target 8-12%)
6. Average loss per loss        : ___  (target 5-7%)
7. Net P&L for the month        : ___
8. Best trade and why           : ___
9. Worst trade and why          : ___
10. What to do differently      : ___
```

**The key ratio:**
```
Risk-Reward Ratio = Average Profit / Average Loss

Example:
Average win  = 10%
Average loss = 5%
Risk-Reward  = 2:1

With 2:1 risk reward:
Even if you win only 40% of trades → you're profitable

10 trades:
4 winners × 10% = +40%
6 losers  × 5%  = -30%
Net = +10% profit with only 40% win rate ✅
```

This is why stop losses matter so much. They keep your losses small so your winners can outweigh them mathematically.

ONE FINAL HONEST POINT
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Short term trading looks easy from outside. In reality:

```
70% of retail traders lose money in first year
20% break even
10% are consistently profitable

The 10% who succeed:
→ Treat it like a business not a game
→ Follow rules even when emotions say otherwise
→ Review and improve constantly
→ Never risk money they can't afford to lose
→ Start small, scale only after proving profitability
```

Start with paper trading for at least 4 weeks. Prove the system works before risking real money. The market will always be there — there's no rush to jump in before you're ready.
