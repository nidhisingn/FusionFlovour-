======================================================================
                 STOCK MARKET PARAMETERS — QUICK REFERENCE
======================================================================

Use this as a revision sheet while reading financial statements.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
INCOME STATEMENT FLOW (Big Picture)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

REVENUE (Top Line)
  - Cost of Goods Sold
= GROSS PROFIT
  - Operating Expenses (salaries, rent, marketing)
= EBITDA
  - Depreciation & Amortization
= EBIT
  - Interest
= EBT
  - Tax
= NET PROFIT / PAT (Bottom Line)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1) REVENUE
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Total money earned from selling products/services

Key points:
  • Recorded when sale happens (accrual), not when cash is received
  • Includes operating + non-operating revenue (asset sales, investments)
  • Formula: Units Sold × Selling Price
  • High revenue alone is meaningless if expenses grow faster

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2) EBITDA & EBITDA MARGIN
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, Amortization
  • Shows core operational profitability (removes financing + accounting noise)

Formula:
  • EBITDA = Revenue − Operating Expenses (before D&A, interest, tax)
  • EBITDA Margin = EBITDA ÷ Revenue × 100

Rules of thumb:
  • Higher margin => more operationally efficient
  • Typical margins: IT 20–35% | FMCG 15–25% | Retail 5–10%
  • Compare within the same sector only

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3) NET PROFIT & NET MARGIN
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Final profit after suppliers, employees, lenders, and government
  • Also called PAT (Profit After Tax) / Bottom Line

Formula:
  • Net Profit = EBITDA − Depreciation − Interest − Tax
  • Net Margin = Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

Signal:
  • Consistently growing net profit = healthy business

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4) EQUITY (Shareholders' Equity)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • What shareholders own after all debts are paid

Formula:
  • Equity = Total Assets − Total Liabilities

Components:
  • Share Capital + Retained Earnings + Reserves & Surplus

Analogy:
  • House worth ₹50L, loan ₹30L -> Your equity = ₹20L

----------------------------------------------------------------------
5) EPS (Earnings Per Share)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Profit attributable to each share

Formula:
  • EPS = Net Profit ÷ Total Shares Outstanding

Signal:
  • Higher EPS => more profitable per share
  • EPS growing YoY => very bullish
  • EPS is the base for P/E ratio

----------------------------------------------------------------------
6) P/E RATIO (Price to Earnings)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • How much investors pay for every ₹1 of earnings

Formula:
  • P/E = Stock Price ÷ EPS

Interpretation:
  • High P/E => high growth expected (or overvalued)
  • Low P/E  => undervalued (or weak growth expected)

Rules:
  • Compare only within same sector
  • Meaningless for loss-making companies

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7) P/B RATIO (Price to Book)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • How much investors pay for every ₹1 of net assets

Formula:
  • P/B = Stock Price ÷ Book Value Per Share

Interpretation:
  • P/B < 1  => below asset value (undervalued or in trouble)
  • P/B 1–3  => fair range
  • P/B > 5  => high growth expectations

Best for:
  • Banks, NBFCs, insurance companies

----------------------------------------------------------------------
8) ROE (Return on Equity)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Efficiency of shareholder capital

Formula:
  • ROE = Net Profit ÷ Shareholders' Equity × 100

Rule of thumb:
  • <10% weak | 15–20% good | >20% excellent

Warning:
  • High ROE can be inflated by high debt. Check ROE with D/E.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
9) D/E RATIO (Debt to Equity)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Debt level relative to shareholder capital

Formula:
  • D/E = Total Debt ÷ Total Equity

Rule of thumb:
  • <0.5 very safe | 0.5–1.5 moderate | >2 risky | >3 red flag

Context:
  • Capital-heavy sectors (infra/real estate) naturally run higher D/E
  • Compare within same sector

----------------------------------------------------------------------
10) FREE CASH FLOW (FCF)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Real cash generated after maintaining the business

Formula:
  • FCF = Operating Cash Flow − Capital Expenditure

Why it matters:
  • "Profit is opinion. Cash is fact."
  • Positive FCF => dividends, buybacks, debt repayment, acquisitions
  • Negative FCF in a mature company => warning

Extra:
  • FCF Yield = FCF ÷ Market Cap × 100

----------------------------------------------------------------------
11) BETA
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Volatility relative to the overall market

Interpretation:
  • Beta = 1   -> moves with market
  • Beta > 1   -> more volatile (higher risk/reward)
  • Beta < 1   -> more stable (defensive)
  • Beta < 0   -> moves opposite to market

----------------------------------------------------------------------
12) 52-WEEK HIGH / LOW
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Highest/lowest price in the past 1 year

Interpretation:
  • Near 52W high -> strong momentum (confirm fundamentals)
  • Near 52W low  -> potential opportunity (investigate WHY it fell)

Key question:
  • Business performance issue or market sentiment?

----------------------------------------------------------------------
13) MARKET CAPITALIZATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Total market value of the company

Formula:
  • Market Cap = Stock Price × Total Shares Outstanding

Buckets (rough):
  • Large Cap  > ₹20,000 Cr (stable, lower risk)
  • Mid Cap    ₹5,000–20,000 Cr (growth + moderate risk)
  • Small Cap  < ₹5,000 Cr (high growth + high risk)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
14) DIVIDEND YIELD
----------------------------------------------------------------------
What it means:
  • Annual cash return from dividends relative to price

Formula:
  • Dividend Yield = Annual Dividend Per Share ÷ Stock Price × 100

Notes:
  • Useful for income-focused investors
  • High yield + falling stock can be a trap (check the reason)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
NON-CASH & TIMING CONCEPTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Depreciation:
  • Expense recorded yearly; cash left earlier when asset was bought
  • Non-cash -> added back in cash flow

Receivables:
  • Sales booked but cash not received
  • If receivables increase -> subtract in cash flow (cash stuck)

Payables:
  • Expenses booked but cash not paid
  • If payables increase -> add back in cash flow (cash retained)

Inventory:
  • Cash spent on goods not yet sold
  • If inventory increases -> subtract in cash flow (cash locked)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
TAXES (Quick Reference)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Corporate Tax  : ~22–25% on company profits
MAT            : Minimum tax floor, prevents zero-tax situations
Deferred Tax   : Future tax due to timing differences
GST            : Pass-through tax (not deducted from profit)
TDS            : Advance tax deducted at source, adjusted later


----------------------------------------------------------------------
CASH FLOW STATEMENT — 3 SECTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Operating Cash Flow:
  • Cash from core operations (most important) -> shows real business health

Investing Cash Flow:
  • Cash spent/received on assets, acquisitions, investments
  • Usually negative for growing companies

Financing Cash Flow:
  • Cash from loans/repayments, share issuance, dividends
  • Shows how company manages its capital

----------------------------------------------------------------------
THE GOLDEN CHECKLIST — Healthy Company
----------------------------------------------------------------------
✓ Revenue growing consistently YoY
✓ EBITDA margin stable or expanding
✓ Net profit growing
✓ ROE above 15–20%
✓ D/E below 1 (or industry-appropriate)
✓ FCF positive and growing
✓ EPS growing every year
✓ Low/manageable interest burden
✓ Receivables not growing faster than revenue
✓ Inventory not piling up

----------------------------------------------------------------------
QUICK MEMORY (1-liners)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
REVENUE     -> What the company EARNS
EBITDA      -> What operations GENERATE
NET PROFIT  -> What shareholders KEEP
EQUITY      -> What shareholders OWN
FCF         -> What the company actually HAS in cash
ROE         -> How EFFICIENTLY equity is used
D/E         -> How RISKY the capital structure is
P/E         -> How EXPENSIVE the stock is
Beta        -> How VOLATILE the stock is

----------------------------------------------------------------------
ONE LINE RULE
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Never judge a stock by a single parameter.
Always look at: Revenue + EBITDA Margin + Net Profit + ROE + D/E + FCF
together over at least 3–5 years.
